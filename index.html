Teachings & Resources — Scripture, Safety, and Hope
Ezekiel 34 — God against abusive shepherds

God rebukes shepherds who use the flock instead of serving it and promises, “I myself will shepherd My sheep.” The Lord defends the harmed and removes predatory leaders.

  • Encouragement: Your Good Shepherd sees and will act.
1 Peter 5:2–3 — Gentle leadership

Leaders are to shepherd willingly, not domineering, being examples to the flock. Coercion and control oppose Christ.

Matthew 20:25–28 — Not like the Gentiles

Jesus flips power: leaders serve. Greatness is measured by humility, not platform.

Luke 4:18 — He binds up the brokenhearted

Jesus’ mission includes healing the bruised, freeing the oppressed, and proclaiming good news to the poor. Church wounds do not disqualify you from His care.

Matthew 7:15–20 — Fruit test

False prophets can look right but harm people. Examine consistent fruit: humility, integrity, repentance, love (Gal 5:22–23).

3 John 9–12 — Diotrephes and hospitality

Diotrephes loves to be first, rejects correction, and bullies. God commends those who welcome, serve, and walk in truth.

Practical Safety Steps
  • Keep records: save messages, dates, and witnesses.
  • Set boundaries: no private meetings; bring a support person; insist on written communication.
  • Seek outside counsel: wise pastors, trauma-informed counselors.
  • If a crime may have occurred, contact local authorities.
Finding a Healthy Church
  • Plural leadership with accountability; financial transparency.
  • Teachable leaders who repent and welcome questions.
  • Member care > platform growth; patient discipleship; shared power.
  • Clear, practiced safeguarding policies.

Grounding & Care

  • Place a hand on your chest. Breathe slowly: in 4 • hold 2 • out 6 (repeat 5 times).
  • Psalm 23 aloud: “The Lord is my Shepherd… He restores my soul.”
  • Text one safe person: “Can you check in with me today?”

Info — What this app does

Gentle Shepherd is a loving, pastoral tool for those harmed by church dynamics or spiritual abuse. It helps you build a healing plan, discern red/green flags, replace lies with Scripture’s truth, and learn what God says about shepherds and safety.

How to use

  1. Open Healing Plan, write a kind summary, set boundaries, and choose one gentle step.
  2. Review Red/Green Flags. Notice patterns—your discernment is valuable.
  3. Use Lie → Truth to rewrite harmful messages with God’s word. Read them aloud with the Voice you choose.
  4. Explore Resources for Scripture and safety practices.

Install on your phone

  • iPhone (Safari): Share → Add to Home Screen
  • Android (Chrome): Menu ⋮ → Add to Home Screen
